The Process

Disrupting Momentum with Two Hands

One of the most important discoveries during the process was a movement score that eventually became the signature movement in the piece.

The score emerged from my background as a freestyle and hip-hop dancer. When I freestyle, I often look for ways to interrupt my own habits and disrupt patterns before they become predictable. During one improvisation session, I noticed that whenever I wanted to stop myself from continuing a movement pathway, I would place both hands on a part of my body and use that point of contact to redirect myself somewhere else.

I began exploring this idea further and eventually developed a score that I called Disrupting Momentum with Two Hands. (Will be mentioned as “The score” hereafter)

The rules are - Movement continues until both hands arrive at the same point on the body. Once both hands make contact, that point generates a new direction or impulse. The body then follows that impulse until the body is interrupted again. As the score developed, I expanded it beyond the body so that performers could also place both hands at a point in space and allow that imagined point to generate movement.

What interested me most about the score was its adaptability. Although the structure remained the same, the quality of the movement could change completely depending on how it was approached. This meant that the same score could exist throughout the entire choreography while taking on different characteristics in each stage of the cycle.

It was present in creation, preservation, destruction, illusion, and release, but transformed according to the qualities of each section. Looking back, this score became one of the most significant choreographic tools I developed throughout the project because it allowed a single movement principle to evolve alongside the larger ideas of the work.
