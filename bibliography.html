Influences and References

My research into Hindu texts, translations of Vedic ideas, and contemporary writings on Natraj led me to the concept of the five acts of the cosmic dance: creation, preservation, destruction, illusion, and release.

Deborah Hay's What if... practice influenced my approach to generating and developing material. This allowed movement material, scores, and choreographic structures to emerge through exploration rather than predetermined outcomes.

Matthias Sperling's ideas around daily practice, embodied thinking, and choreographic knowledge informed my understanding of movement generation.

Jem Finer's Longplayer provided a reference for thinking about duration and endlessness. The idea of a work that exists within an ongoing cycle rather than moving towards a definitive ending.

A direct choreographic reference for the Preservation section was Siobhan Davies' Rotor. The work's use of walking, repetition, and continually shifting relationships between performers informed my exploration of collective movement systems.
